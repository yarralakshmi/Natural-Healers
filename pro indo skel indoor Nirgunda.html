ü™¥The Introduction of Nirgundi

Nirgundi (*Vitex negundo*), commonly known as the Five-leaved Chaste Tree, is a powerful Ayurvedic herb widely recognized for its benefits to the skeletal system. Native to India, it has been traditionally used to treat joint pain, arthritis, and musculoskeletal inflammation. Rich in anti-inflammatory compounds like flavonoids and terpenoids, Nirgundi helps reduce swelling, ease stiffness, and promote mobility in affected joints. Its leaves and roots are often used in oils, decoctions, or poultices to relieve pain and support bone and muscle recovery. Whether applied topically or consumed in controlled doses, Nirgundi serves as a natural ally in maintaining skeletal strength and flexibility.

Nirgundi (Vitex negundo), commonly known as the Five-leaved Chaste Tree, is a hardy aromatic shrub native to the Indian subcontinent and Southeast Asia. It belongs to the Lamiaceae family and thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often found along riverbanks, forest edges, and wastelands.
