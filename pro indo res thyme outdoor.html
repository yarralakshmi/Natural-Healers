ü™¥The Introduction of Thyme

Thyme (Thymus vulgaris) is a fragrant, woody perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region. It belongs to the mint family (Lamiaceae) and is well-known for its tiny green leaves, strong aroma, and pink or purple flowers. For centuries, thyme has been cherished for both its culinary and medicinal properties. It adds depth of flavor to many dishes and is valued in herbal medicine for its antiseptic, antibacterial, and antioxidant qualities. Easy to grow and drought-tolerant, thyme is also a favorite in home gardens, rockeries, and as a ground cover due to its hardy nature and attractive appearance.
