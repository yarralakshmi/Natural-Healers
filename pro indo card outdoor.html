Terminalia Bellerica (Baheda)

Terminalia Bellerica, commonly known as Baheda, is a large deciduous tree native to India and Southeast Asia. It belongs to the Combretaceae family and is widely recognized for its medicinal value in traditional Ayurvedic medicine. The tree produces greyish fruits that are used for therapeutic purposes and is one of the three ingredients in the famous Ayurvedic formulation Triphala. Baheda is known for its rejuvenating properties and is believed to balance the kapha dosha. It is used in various remedies to support digestion, respiratory health, and cardiovascular function. The fruit is rich in tannins, antioxidants, and essential compounds that help in reducing inflammation and promoting overall wellness. Baheda trees grow well in tropical and subtropical climates, requiring moderate water and full sunlight. They are usually propagated through seeds and are commonly found in forested regions across India.

Ginkgo Biloba

Ginkgo Biloba is one of the oldest living tree species, often referred to as a living fossil, and has been used in traditional medicine for thousands of years. Native to China, it is now cultivated in various parts of the world, including India. The tree is easily recognized by its unique fan-shaped leaves. Ginkgo is primarily known for its ability to enhance blood circulation and protect the cardiovascular system. Its leaves contain powerful antioxidants such as flavonoids and terpenoids, which help reduce oxidative stress and improve vascular health. Ginkgo Biloba is commonly used to support memory and cognitive function, but it also plays a beneficial role in maintaining heart health by improving the flow of blood through arteries and reducing the risk of clot formation. The tree thrives in temperate climates and can be grown outdoors with moderate care and well-drained soil.

Hawthorn (Crataegus spp.)

Hawthorn is a small, thorny tree or shrub belonging to the Crataegus genus, commonly found in temperate regions across Europe, Asia, and North America. It has a long history of use in traditional medicine, particularly for supporting heart health. The plant is recognized for its clusters of white or pink flowers and bright red berries. In herbal medicine, both the leaves, flowers, and berries are used to make extracts and teas. Hawthorn is valued for its ability to strengthen the heart muscle, regulate blood pressure, and improve overall cardiovascular function. It contains beneficial compounds such as flavonoids and oligomeric procyanidins that contribute to its medicinal properties. The plant grows best in well-drained soil with ample sunlight and is suitable for cultivation in various parts of India with mild climates.

Olive Tree (Olea europaea)

The Olive Tree, scientifically known as Olea europaea, is a slow-growing evergreen tree native to the Mediterranean region and now cultivated in various parts of the world, including India. Known for its silvery-green leaves and small green to black fruits, the tree has been valued for centuries for its health-promoting properties. While the fruit is used to produce olive oil, the leaves are also widely used in traditional medicine for their therapeutic benefits. Olive leaves contain oleuropein, a compound known for its cardiovascular and anti-inflammatory effects. The tree thrives in warm, dry climates with plenty of sunlight and well-drained soil, making it suitable for outdoor cultivation in arid and semi-arid regions of India.
