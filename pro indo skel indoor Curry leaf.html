ü™¥The Introduction of Curry leaf

The Curry Leaf plant is a highly aromatic and evergreen shrub native to India and Sri Lanka. It is widely cultivated for its flavorful leaves, which are a staple in Indian cooking. Curry leaf (Murraya koenigii) is native to the Indian subcontinent and belongs to the Rutaceae family, which includes citrus plants. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, especially in well-drained soils with ample sunlight. The plant is prized for its aromatic, glossy green leaves that add depth and flavor to South Asian cuisine. Beyond culinary use, the leaves are known for their medicinal properties, including antioxidant, anti-inflammatory, and antimicrobial effects.

During the medieval period, the spice trade brought South Asian herbs like curry leaf to Arab herbalists and physicians. Though not widely used in Europe at the time, its medicinal reputation grew through Middle Eastern texts and trade networks.

üåé Transatlantic Expansion and Farming

Curry leaf was introduced to Sri Lanka, Southeast Asia, Africa, and the Caribbean during colonial expansions.
