üåøü™ÑEndocrine glands are specialized organs that act as the body‚Äôs hormone factories, producing and secreting hormones directly into the bloodstream. Unlike other glands with ducts, endocrine glands are ductless, allowing hormones to travel freely to various parts of the body. üî∏üíå Hormones ‚Äì Chemical Messengers

Hormones are powerful chemical messengers produced by endocrine glands and released directly into the bloodstream. They travel throughout the body to reach specific target organs and cells, where they trigger vital biological responses. Each hormone has a unique role, helping regulate functions such as growth, metabolism, mood, reproduction, and homeostasis. For instance, insulin helps control blood sugar levels, thyroxine regulates metabolism, and adrenaline prepares the body for emergency responses. Unlike nerve impulses, hormones work more slowly but have longer-lasting effects. The balance and timing of hormone release are carefully controlled to keep the body functioning properly. Even a tiny amount of a hormone can create a significant response, showing just how essential these chemical messengers are in maintaining overall health and balance.
