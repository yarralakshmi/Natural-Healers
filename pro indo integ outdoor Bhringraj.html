It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is commonly found in moist, marshy areas, often thriving during the monsoon season. Bhringraj has been extensively used in Ayurvedic and Siddha medicine for thousands of years. In Ayurveda, Bhringraj is believed to balance the pitta and kapha doshas and was used in oil infusions, powders, and decoctions. Ancient physicians often combined it with other herbs to treat systemic issues from the inside out, particularly through blood purification and liver support.

üèõÔ∏è Recognition in Folk and Regional Medicine

In Indian folk traditions, Bhringraj was a go-to remedy for everything from hair loss to skin infections and minor wounds. The plant was often made into a paste and used to soothe burns or insect bites. In other traditional systems like Unani and Chinese medicine, Bhringraj was also used for its cooling and detoxifying nature. Its wide availability and fast growth made it a staple in village medicine cabinets and a symbol of natural, accessible healthcare.
