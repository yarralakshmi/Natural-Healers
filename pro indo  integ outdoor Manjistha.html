Manjistha, scientifically known as Rubia cordifolia, is a perennial climbing plant native to the Himalayan region and other tropical and subtropical parts of Asia. It belongs to the Rubiaceae family and is easily recognized by its slender, twining stems and vibrant reddish roots, which are the most valued part of the plant. Often found growing along forest edges, riverbanks, and hill slopes, Manjistha thrives in moist, fertile soil. In Indian folk medicine, Manjistha was commonly used as a natural dye as well as a medicinal herb. Being a climbing or spreading vine, Manjistha requires support such as trellises, fences, or nearby shrubs to grow vertically and avoid tangling on the ground. Manjistha roots are harvested after 18 to 24 months when they have fully matured and developed their characteristic reddish color.
